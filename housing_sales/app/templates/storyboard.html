Chapter 01

The Housing Market Landscape

King County, Washington saw 21,588 residential property sales between May 2014 and May 2015. Average sale prices and monthly volume fluctuated with seasonality and market conditions.

📈
Avg Sale Price: $538K — with median at $450K, indicating a right-skewed distribution driven by luxury properties.
🗓
Peak Sales Month: Spring 2015 — May showed the highest sales volume, consistent with seasonal real estate trends.
💲
Price Range: $78K – $4.67M — a wide spectrum reflecting diverse neighbourhoods, sizes, and conditions.

Chapter 02

Does Renovation Pay Off?

Only 4.2% of King County homes were ever renovated — yet those that were commanded a significant price premium over never-renovated counterparts.

🔨
+41% Price Premium — Renovated properties averaged $707K vs $500K for never-renovated homes.
📅
Recency Matters — Properties renovated within the last 10 years command even higher premiums than older renovations.
💡
Strategic Insight — Renovation ROI is highest for mid-age (30–50 yr) homes. Older homes benefit most from modern updates.

Chapter 03

Age Tells a Story

The average King County home is 44 years old. How does a property's age affect its market value and distribution across segments?

🏚
Old homes (50+ yrs) dominate the market with the highest count, yet their avg price varies widely based on condition & location.
✨
New homes (<10 yrs) are rarest but command premium prices due to modern construction standards and energy efficiency.
🎯
Sweet spot: Mid-age (10–30 yrs) — these balance modern features with established neighbourhood value.

Chapter 04

Room by Room Impact

Bedrooms, bathrooms, and floors each contribute uniquely to a property's market value. Understanding these relationships is key to accurate pricing.

🛏
4–5 bedrooms deliver the strongest price premium. Beyond 6, price can decline due to smaller per-room sqft.
🚿
3+ bathrooms show a 2× price multiple vs 1-bathroom homes, making bathrooms a high-ROI renovation target.
🏗
2.5-floor properties peak in avg price, likely representing high-end homes with a bonus loft or finished basement.
